  How iOS Apps Work (The Basics)

  To get an app on your iPhone (not a website), you have a few options:
  Method: Xcode Direct Install
  Cost: Free
  How it Works: Connect iPhone to Mac, run from Xcode. App expires after 7 days,

    need to reinstall. Good for development.

  Method: TestFlight
  Cost: $99/year (Apple Developer)
  How it Works: Install via TestFlight app. Lasts 90 days. Can share with
  others.
  ────────────────────────────────────────
  Method: App Store
  Cost: $99/year + Apple Review
  How it Works: Public app. We don't need this.
  For personal use, Xcode Direct Install is fine - you just reconnect your phone
   to your Mac once a week and hit "Run" again.

  ---
  What "Offline Support" Means

  Without offline support: App only works when you have internet. Open app →
  fetches reminders from n8n → displays them.

  With offline support: App saves reminders locally on your phone. Can view/edit
   even without internet. Syncs when connection returns.

  For your use case, no offline support is fine - your phone is almost always
  connected, and reminders need to sync anyway.
